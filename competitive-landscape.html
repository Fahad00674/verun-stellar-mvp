Competitive Landscape Brief

Verun vs. The AI-Agent Trust Layer

A high-level comparison of who else is building "trust + identity for AI agents on-chain" — and how each one makes money.
Prepared for: Fahad Farooq · BCP Partners GmbH · May 2026

TL;DR — 30-Second Read

The space went from "open" to crowded between Q4 2025 and Q1 2026. ERC-8004 went live on Ethereum mainnet on Jan 29, 2026 and became the de-facto standard (backed by MetaMask, Ethereum Foundation, Google, Coinbase). KYA ("Know Your Agent") is now the regulatory term — Trulioo, Worldpay, Visa, Mastercard, Skyfire are all in. Verun's mechanism (validator consensus + SBT + on-chain anchor) is no longer unique, but its EU-regulatory framing, protocol-custodial design, and opinionated 2-of-3 consensus are still defensible — if repositioned quickly as an ERC-8004-compatible compliance layer for EU regulated entities.

Earning Models — Verun's Realistic Options

Verun is the trust layer, not the money layer. That rules out interchange / transaction-percentage models (which is where Skyfire, Visa, Mastercard make their margin). Three viable models, in order of EU-fit:

1 · Per-verification fee (B2B API)

€0.05 – €0.50 per verdict

tokenforge and other regulated entities pay per /api/evaluate call. Stellar anchor cost is sub-cent, so margin is high. Same model as Trulioo & RNWY — proven.

2 · Enterprise compliance license

€20K – €200K / year

Annual contract with EU-regulated firms (banks, MiFID II investment firms, MiCA CASPs). Includes SLA, audit dashboard, kill-switch, custom validator pool. Higher-value, lower-volume — fits the regulation pitch.

3 · Anchor-as-a-Service

€0.001 per anchor + fixed monthly

Sell the on-chain anchor (memo-hash) to other KYA platforms that need an immutable audit trail but don't want to operate Stellar accounts. Verun becomes "the notary" inside someone else's stack.

Strategic Verdict

Don't compete with ERC-8004 — be a recognised implementation of it. Verun's 2-of-3 consensus is a concrete answer to ERC-8004's deliberately-unopinionated Validation Registry, and that is genuinely useful to EU regulated buyers who need a defined, auditable validator pool — not a "pick-your-own-validator" framework.

Lean into the EU-compliance moat. MiCA Art. 68, AI Act Art. 14, and MiFID II Art. 17 mapping is something almost no US-led competitor (RNWY, Skyfire, Trulioo's commercial pitch) leads with. The EU AI Act high-risk requirements take full effect Aug 2, 2026 — that's a 90-day window to be the go-to "EU-native, ERC-8004-compatible, protocol-custodial verifier."

Recommended pricing wedge: Model 2 (enterprise compliance license) for the first 2–3 design partners (BCP, tokenforge, one bank), then open Model 1 (per-verification API) for general developer access. Model 3 only if a partnership with another KYA platform materialises.
